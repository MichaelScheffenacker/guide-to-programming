In Mathematics symbols are thrown together in a disrespectful manner. I mean look at it: who could ever make out, what this letters and signs suppose to be.  They do not even constitute anything of meaning.  Sure they might represent numbers, somehow.  But those numbers do not represent anything either.  So what can possibly be the purpose of this symbol mash.  Sure somehow this is supposed to be the foundation of gigantic bridges, vast [technology] and microscopic computers.  But this can not possibly be right, how can this arbitrary thing be the foundation of anything?
The first thing to learn to understand Mathematics is to learn to read the formulas properly.  Mathematical formulas are extremely dense and precise pieces of information.  In our every day languages we are not used to this kind of density and precision.  We are used to give and receive redundant information.  In a normal conversation we say everything twice, so somebody who understands only half of the said, will still be able to reconstruct the underlying information.  We are so used to doing so we tend to not realize it, but it is in our words, in our grammar and in our thought structures.  One example of redundancy is double negation, which is common in colloquial speech but rejected in formality; and only this discrepancy of colloquial and formal use makes us aware of this particular form of redundancy while all the other forms stay unconscious.
Mathematics removes all redundancy from its formalisms, this makes it very hard to read for the untrained eye. Every single symbol is crucial and if you miss only one, you likely do not only get to a wrong result but to a disastrously wrong result. Therefore mathematical formulas have to be written down by hand, carefully, on a piece of paper, symbol by symbol before they can be considered read.
Another difficulty with mathematical notation is, that the symbols do not always mean the same thing; the meaning might be context dependent or explicitly defined differently at different places.

In Algebra the equal sign `=` balances the equation: the left term and the right term ought to be the same.
```
a + b = ab
```
But this is not the only meaning of the equal sign:  It is also used to assign a value to a variable.
```
a = 5
b = a
```
To distinguish this meaning from the balancing one, there is often another symbol used to signalize the assignment: `:=`
```
a := 5
b := a
```
Since this assignment sign is not symmetrical anymore, it might also allow to assign in the other direction, from left to right:
```
a =:b
```
